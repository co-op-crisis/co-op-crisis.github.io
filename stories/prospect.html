The article talks about common challenges faced by residents of co-op buildings in New York City. It covers issues such as conflicts with neighbors, building management problems, financial concerns, and difficulties in selling or subletting apartments. These challenges can be exacerbated by the unique structure of co-op ownership, where residents own shares in the building rather than individual units. The article provides advice on how co-op residents can navigate these issues and protect their interests within the complex dynamics of co-op living in NYC.
